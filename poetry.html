Poetry


clear to the ones who care to ruminate
ludicracrous to the ones who are just passersby



will time
rub away
the passion and desires
that once ran wild in my heart




you sung the world into creation
and orchestrated our heartbeats
to vibrate
with your spirit




scared by the superfluity of my own ideas
teemingly jealous of those who have already narrowed
a paucity of possibilities
early in life




a desire to be understood
to reveal my vulnerability
in careful measures
in order to connect deeper




your roots are the hidden seeds
once planted but forgotten
until you’ve searched the world and realized
that what you’ve always wished you had
was already within you




love is measured in how much you're willing to sacrifice



are you afraid of intimacy
of getting close to your own heart?
do you hear your heartbeat change, jumping at unexpected intervals
as you’re getting ready for a date with yourself?


when was the last time you truly wrote
the last time you played with words for words alone?
have you been so confined by the literary constraints marked by your society
that you’ve forgotten that they were marked with dry-erased markers
capable of being changed and being bent?
words can exist outside of their conventional boundaries.
in fact, words are the most effective when they’re matched from unexpected places.


would you still write
if there’s no immediate audience?
would you still write
if there’s no instantaneous approval?


with age, you’ve grown in knowledge
of literary usage, for validation’s sake and nothing more
with age, you’ve lost the will of expressing what’s truly on your heart
untainted and unpolished
the kind that children spurt out and is either met with a chuckle or an apology to the adults
with age, you’ve learned that venturing into uncharted territories is a set up for pitfall
with age, you’ve wrestled with the thought of whether it is morally right and selfish to put yourself first
when there is so much suffering in the world, so much undistributed wealth
with age, you turn to kids
their unfathomable laughters at everyday things
and wonder
is ignorance is truly bliss?



he knows the crevices of our hearts.
the irritable boulder that lies between
our current reality
and where we want to be
he reveals
when we silence ourselves
for silencing a restless heart
takes defeat

an end to ourselves
is the beginning of his work in us

it is your handiwork that sculpts me
an iteration after every setback
my final version unknown
